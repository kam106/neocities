Crafting the ad

Okay, let's try this again.
While Reddit might seam like a wonderful tool for finding servers, you have to remember that you're basically advertising yourself to server owners. At least, that's what it's like where I'm from. So if you like the simple part of just having a new server fly on your face while you grab the ad and say "Oh my god, my calling has come to me!", you're wrong. Here's how it actually is.

First off you have to literally write out what you're looking for. In other words, basically crafting your own ads. Start with your age, because for one, who doesn't already share their age, and for two, because apparently some servers only allow people who are over 18 and over 16. Yes, those things exist. Because somebody in the Minecraft server community decided "Hey, this should be a meta!".

Now that you have your age and a basic intro paragraph (the part nobody cares about), you have to actually explain the shit you want. Be it a bunch of fighting people, some exploring, or even something basic. But don't get yourself too caught up, because this is where things kind of begin to fall apart.

You probably expected a simple game, didn't you? To finally easily find the server you want, and only the people who think they know you drop by, giving you servers you specify... You sure want that simple, vanilla server, with nothing but TPA, huh? Well fuck you, because you're getting Towny and Factions and you'll like it.

The "MinecraftBuddies" Effect

This is a strange trend I've noticed a lot with these kind of things. You list out in detail that you don't like factions because it's all about fighting and it's an RPG thing and you don't like the conflict it makes, then bam, come join our factions heavy server. This has happened nearly consistently in my own experience, and the fact that this happens basically almost EVERY time I'm searching for a server is fucking crazy.

You could be a mindless zombie, sure. You can stick to scrolling the server lists, but in the end, most of the things you see will just be ass and you won't like any of it. So instead of scrolling servers you usually don't like (at least in my case), you resort to letting people come to you and instead of getting better luck, people spit on you by ignoring half of your post.

Seriously, it does not take any effort to read I don't want FACTIONS of all things. What, am I being recommended Factions because I mentioned it once? Do people really miss the "I'm not looking for" part next to factions? Really?

Okay cool, so that part doesn't matter. So you write something dumb like "Only looking for Vanilla servers". Okay cool, you got your age, you got a short introduction, you got written what you're looking for. But uh oh, school's coming up soon and almost every teacher at your school will surely love giving you homework!

And then surprise, you'll have to do it all again, because little Timmy over there wants you to join his server, but then you have to be active 24/7 to stay. Yeah, real thing, wouldn't you love being active more than 3 hours for 5 days a week on Timmy's server like you're working a part time, right?
