History's Introduction

Okay, so what makes a normal ROBLOX event fun? Well, they should last maybe a month, they should have a good selection of games, and you shouldn't be left confused and lost. The more memorable the event the better, but normally that doesn't make a difference.

Now, The Hunt: First Edition? It at least made an attempt, but it overwhelmed people with it's 100 games, did easter events in a style people found lazy ("dev hunts"), most of the items ended up being really expensive and event exclusive, and there was very little free prizes among other things. Despite those issues, The Hunt was a return to ROBLOX events and while it had many flaws, some that completely ruined the event, it still had potential and needed some serious feedback to be improved.

And The Classic?
Worse than the last event (subjectively). Hear me out.

Not only in a way that's possibly unfair (i.e: completing a whole minigame with crap teammates, super high difficulty etc), but also you only get a week to do it! Why put in effort when there is no effort? That's why I tried it for a few hours, then didn't bother. The grind wasn't even worth it. Lazy of me, sure, but wouldn't that make ROBLOX just as lazy of a company for making this garbage? We gave lots of feedback, did they not listen to all of it?

I get that the timeframe celebrates history and it's the time between the site's reaction and trademarks being made, but that's only 5 days. Nobody's going to stomach this entire event in five days! At least, I'm not...

Let's also add on the fact most people will be busy with school and work and whatnot. So not only is it too short by regular standards, you leave basically no time for anybody who's actually studying right now! If this isn't the shortsighted part of the event...
ROBLOX. You are a KIDS game. If the KIDS are busy studying, why would you engineer this event during this time? THINK.

Not only that, but it also has the same issues as the last event. Most of the good items that were showcased were either pay high amounts to get, or they ended up being extremely tedious to get. Inconsistencies, Ahoy!


Now, I am not by any means an older player. Depending on who you ask, either I'm older than most of ROBLOX's active playerbase, or I'm not even close to being an 'older' player. But just hear me out.
I think ROBLOX is not properly doing the classic theme. Let me explain.

Since many active people played past 2016, let's start with the things you're more likely to notice:
First off, the UI takes modern design hints. The mute music button uses an emoji, and is placed by the modern icons. Now that would be cool in any other game, but this is a classic themed event. You couldn't have hand drawn an icon? What about doing it how old games did and using the text "Mute Music"? At the least, using old color schemes, like 2013+'s blue and grey, or even 2012's red and black?
Less noticably, dialogs and chat. While dialog textures the same as they were in 2015 and later on, they still use 2022+ chat bubbles. The opposite also applies to chat, how the chat box is the modern chat box, but they could of used the old chat box, WHICH BY THE WAY, IS STILL SUPPORTED.
I can only assume this was likely a corporate decision. I could say a bunch of reasons why this happened, such as corporate greed, or just a dev team who had no idea about ROBLOX's 18 year history... Holy shit, it's going to have been 18 years in September.

Anyway, there's also safe chat. Of course, they didn't remove chat for people younger than 13... That would be dumb. But they added that little chat bubble from 2008 where you click options and there's buttons... That thing. But they don't have most of the old dialogs, so it feels more like it's there for novelty rather than accuracy. But at least we have old games featured...
